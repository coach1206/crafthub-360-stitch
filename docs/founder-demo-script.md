# Founder Demo Script — CraftHub 360 / SmokeCraft

A natural, conversational script for walking a venue owner, investor, or
reviewer through the product in about 3–5 minutes. Pause for questions
wherever it feels natural — this isn't meant to be read word-for-word.

---

**Opening (30 seconds)**

"What I want to show you is CraftHub 360 — and specifically the part that's
fully built out today, SmokeCraft, which is the cigar experience. The idea
is simple: instead of a guest just walking into a lounge and picking a cigar
off a shelf, we give them a guided journey — and we give the venue a real
operations layer underneath it."

**The guest journey (90 seconds)**

"So let's walk through it the way a guest would. They start with intake —
basic identity and preferences. From there they move into discovery: cigar
origins, leaf types, flavor profiles — all real content, not placeholder
copy. Then they hit Seed & Soil Pairing, which connects their preferences to
actual growing regions and soil types.

Then comes Mentor Selection — this is one of my favorite parts. The guest
picks two mentors from real tobacco traditions: Dominican Republic,
Nicaragua, Honduras, Mexico, Cuba, Peru, USA, Brazil — each with their own
flag, their own story, their own specialty. That's not decoration, that
shapes the rest of their session.

After that, they can upload media into their Passport — and the Passport
itself is the layer that persists across visits. Stamps, connections,
benefits — it's what turns one visit into a relationship."

**The operations layer (60–90 seconds)**

"Now flip to the staff side. Behind all of that guest experience is a full
floor operations system — we call it POS3 and E.A.T. Table management,
kitchen and bar displays, humidor control, inventory, reorders, staff and
section assignment, reports. Eleven POS3 screens, fourteen E.A.T. screens —
all built, all navigable, all interactive today.

I want to be upfront about where these stand: right now, POS3 and E.A.T. run
on local data on the device — they're not yet talking to a live database
across multiple devices. We've built the pattern that already works for the
guest journey side, and the next phase is literally just wiring these
screens to that same kind of backend. You'll actually see a 'Backend
Pending' label right on these screens — we don't hide that, because I'd
rather you trust everything I tell you than oversell something that isn't
there yet."

**Why it matters for a venue (30 seconds)**

"For a venue, the value is twofold: guests get a premium, memorable
experience that feels built specifically for a cigar lounge — not a generic
POS with a cigar skin — and staff get a real command layer for running the
floor. The Passport piece is what gives you a long-term retention and
loyalty mechanism, not just a single transaction."

**Closing (15 seconds)**

"So that's where we are: the guest experience and the authentication/role
system are genuinely real today. The staff operations layer is fully built
on the front end and ready to wire to a live backend next. I wanted to show
you exactly what's working and exactly what's next, honestly, because that's
how I'd want to be shown something if I were sitting where you are."

---

**If asked "is this live / in production?"**

"Not yet — this is a fully built prototype. The guest journey has a real
backend already. The staff operations side (POS3/E.A.T.) needs its backend
wiring done next, and I can walk you through exactly what that involves if
you want the technical detail."

**If asked "what's the next step?"**

"Wiring POS3 and E.A.T. to the database, connecting SMS/email for upload
links, adding secure media storage, and a final production QA pass. None of
that is new invention — it's replicating a pattern that already works on the
guest side."
